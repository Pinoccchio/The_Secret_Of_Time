## Historical Context Notes

### Pre-Colonial Philippines (Chapter 1)
- Thriving trade networks with China, India, Middle East
- Advanced goldsmithing and metallurgy
- Baybayin script in common use
- Babaylan held significant spiritual and political power
- Women had equal property and divorce rights

### Philippine Revolution (Chapter 2)
- Katipunan founded 1892 by Andres Bonifacio
- Revolution began August 1896
- Used actual coded messages for communication
- Cry of Pugad Lawin marked the start
- Bonifacio executed 1897 due to internal conflicts

### World War II (Chapter 3)
- Bataan Death March: April 1942, 60,000-80,000 prisoners
- Filipino guerrilla movements throughout occupation
- Liberation of Manila: February 1945
- Battle of Manila destroyed 100,000+ civilian lives
- Filipino resistance was crucial to Allied victory

### Martial Law Era (Chapter 4)
- Declared September 21, 1972 by Ferdinand Marcos
- 3,257 known extrajudicial killings
- 35,000 documented torture victims
- 70,000 incarcerated
- Underground press and student movements
- Ninoy Aquino assassinated August 21, 1983

### EDSA Revolution (Chapter 5)
- February 22-25, 1986
- Estimated 2 million participants
- Peaceful People Power revolution
- Marcos fled February 25, 1986
- Corazon Aquino became president
- Radio Veritas played crucial role
